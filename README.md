# 📊 French Labour Market EDA — Python Project

**Author:** Salwa Dhouibi
**Tools:** Python (pandas, matplotlib, seaborn) · Jupyter Notebook
**Dataset:** Public data based on INSEE / Eurostat figures  
**Level:** Intermediate

---

## 🎯 Objective

Perform an end-to-end Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on French unemployment trends. This project combines economics insights with data analysis skills to extract meaningful trends from 2010 to 2023.

---

## 📊 Key Findings & Visualizations

### 1. National Unemployment Trend (2010-2023)
The national rate dropped significantly from its peak of 10.4% in 2013 down to 7.3% in 2023, showing a strong post-COVID recovery.

<img width="1289" height="590" alt="dashboard:chart1_trend" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/e003ff1f-bacd-4327-a503-b3648fe75824" />


### 2. Unemployment by Age Group
Youth unemployment (15-24) remains a structural issue, consistently sitting 2 to 3 times higher than the national average, even though it improved to 17.4% in 2023.

<img width="1189" height="590" alt="dashboard:chart2_age" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/88f4cb6d-de6a-4533-ac7d-2b4cfac2bfef" />


### 3. Gender Gap Disparities
The gap between men and women has nearly closed over the decade, reaching a narrow 0.6 percentage point difference in 2023 (7.0% for men vs 7.6% for women).

<img width="1289" height="590" alt="dashboard:chart3_gender" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/3aebc2dc-5ca7-4e0c-86fc-c6c056c885ce" />


### 4. Regional Disparities
We observe major geographical gaps across France. **Hauts-de-France** suffers from the highest rate (11.3%), while **Bretagne** shows the best performance at 5.8%.
